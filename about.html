About the Project

How the Partnership Works

Since 2023, this work has been a collaboration with the Historic Jackson Ward Association (HJWA), led by its president, Janis Allen. The premise is easy to state and harder to practice: the community identifies what it needs, and the research builds the capacity to address it.

That model was not there at the start. The first project grew out of a 1950 census mapping effort connected to the City of Richmond, and over the following years the work shifted so that HJWA's priorities set the agenda. Each project now begins from a question that matters to the neighborhood's advocacy rather than from a research plan brought in from outside. Students come into Jackson Ward through HJWA meetings, community events, and walking tours led by residents. Proposals are developed through those conversations and brought back to the community for direction, not delivered as finished products for approval.

When the 2025 heat analysis pointed to a planting site on N 2nd Street, for example, the community confirmed and claimed the location, and decided the new trees would carry plaques honoring three of Jackson Ward's own luminaries. That decision came from the neighborhood, not the research team. It is a small illustration of the larger commitment: the work answers to the community, and the measure of its success is whether it produces something the neighborhood can actually use.

The Community Partner

The Historic Jackson Ward Association

Preserving Richmond's historic Jackson Ward

Founded in the 1980s, the Historic Jackson Ward Association represents the residents of the neighborhood and is committed to building awareness of its history, preserving this significant Richmond district, and integrating that history into the modern-day experience. The association works alongside residents, city officials, and local institutions to protect a neighborhood whose National Register inventory lists more than 600 significant historic structures, and which holds more cast-iron work than any neighborhood outside New Orleans.

HJWA sets the direction of this work. The neighborhood's priorities, sites, and sources define the questions each project takes on, keeping the research grounded in what matters most to the people of Jackson Ward.

Students & the University

Undergraduate Research in the Spatial Analysis Lab

University of Richmond · School of Arts & Sciences

The research is led by Dr. Kyle Redican (University of Florida, previously University of Richmond) and carried out with undergraduate students, much of it produced in the University of Richmond's Spatial Analysis Lab (SAL), where students learn GIS, mapping, and spatial analysis and apply them to real projects. Students bring these geographic and historical tools to the questions the community sets.

The exchange runs both ways. The university offers scholarly capacity and student energy. Jackson Ward offers students something no classroom can: direct experience of the history they are studying, and a place in a living community while they navigate their own education. That reciprocity is the standard the partnership holds itself to.

Other partners have contributed to individual projects along the way, including the City of Richmond, VCU, and MapRVA.
