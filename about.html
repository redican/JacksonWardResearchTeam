About the Project

A Student–Community Partnership

This research was conducted by University of Richmond students during faculty-led summer research, through the Department of Geography, Environment, and Sustainability, in collaboration with the Historic Jackson Ward Association. The project pairs the tools of geographic and historical inquiry with the lived knowledge of the neighborhood's own community organization, bringing students into Jackson Ward to document its history alongside the people working to preserve it.

The University

Department of Geography, Environment & Sustainability

University of Richmond · School of Arts & Sciences

The Department of Geography, Environment, and Sustainability prepares students to explore and understand cultural, bio-physical, and geospatial environments, and the ways they interact and change, empowering students to help shape a more just and sustainable world. Coursework spans urbanization, land use, resource management, and climate, and trains students in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and related geospatial technologies.

The research is produced in the department's Spatial Analysis Lab (SAL), its home base for geospatial work, where students learn GIS, mapping, and spatial analysis and apply them to real-world projects. The Jackson Ward project gave students hands-on experience mapping, archiving, and interpreting the history and environment of one of Richmond's most significant neighborhoods.

The Community Partner

The Historic Jackson Ward Association

Preserving Richmond's historic Jackson Ward

Founded in the 1980s, the Historic Jackson Ward Association represents the residents of the neighborhood and is committed to building awareness of its history, preserving this significant Richmond district, and integrating that history into the modern-day experience. The association works alongside residents, city officials, and local institutions to protect a neighborhood whose National Register inventory lists more than 600 significant historic structures, and which holds more cast-iron work than any neighborhood outside New Orleans.

Their partnership grounded this research in the community itself, guiding students toward the stories, sites, and sources that matter most to the people of Jackson Ward.
